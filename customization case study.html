Project Name: Controller Customization Improvements
Roles: Researcher
UX Designer
Year: 2018



Providing a Better Customization Experience

Scuf Gaming is a company that provides customized video game controllers that are enhanced for esports with their patented paddle technology, more colorful button options, and other features.


Problem

The initial task was to improve the mobile builder for a richer customization experience. We’ve noticed that a large majority of our web traffic has shifted to mobile and our building experience is more suited for desktops.


In order to refine the problem more I looked for more hard data. Consulting with the IT department revealed a more concrete problem. According to our heat maps, customers were missing elements due to having to scroll vertically. Due to this, the business was losing revenue since those missed components could range from $3.95 to $14.95 per added part. Also users would constantly have to scroll back and forth to see changes. I hypothesized being able to be focused on the controller would eliminate that problem and provide a more delightful experience.


Competitive Analysis

I compared our experience to other controller customization sites such as Microsoft, Cinch, and Battlebeaver. I noticed that organizing the vast amount of options is a challenge to several companies in this sector. Also noted that the Microsoft experience had a horizontal layout to take advantage of the limited space in mobile and better streamline the experience.


User Research

I interviewed five people while they went through the builder experience. Two were familiar with our builder experience and three were not. All five noted that the scrolling back and forth to see changes to their controller build was tedious and removing that improved their experience.

High Fidelity Mockups

I captured images from our current builder and reworked them to highlight how a more horizontally oriented navigation experience could look for our site.


Conclusion

After presenting my research our Ecommerce Marketing Director and Senior Digital Project Manager, both agreed these were valuable insights. Currently we’re working on an overhaul to the builder experience and my suggestions have been implemented for the new version.
